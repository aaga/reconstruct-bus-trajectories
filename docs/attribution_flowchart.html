Delay attribution algorithm

As implemented in the phone-vs-archive comparison pipeline (the delay_decomposition stack). Inputs assumed: the bus's smoothed speed at every time/distance, and the location of every stop, signal and crossing.

Thresholds used (plain numbers): slow = under 5 mph · speed sampled every 2 s · minimum slow-down length 10 s · merge slow-downs closer than 20 m · dwell zone 30 m before → 10 m after a stop (trimmed at intersections) · crossing approach 50 m before → 10 m after · signal approach 91 m (300 ft) before → 10 m after · near-side = signal within 90 ft (27 m) ahead of a stop.

  Start["INPUTS — the bus's smooth speed at every time and distance,
and the location of every bus stop, traffic signal,
pedestrian signal, crossing and stop sign along the route"]:::io --> S1

  subgraph SEG["1 · Build segments"]
    direction TB
    S1["Split the route into segments — each segment runs from one
signal to the next (traffic signals AND pedestrian signals are boundaries)"]
    S2{"At least two
signals on the route?"}
    S3["STOP — no delays can be attributed"]:::stop
    S4["Mark a bus stop as NEAR-SIDE if a signal lies within
90 ft (27 m) just AHEAD of it, in the direction of travel"]
    S5["Assign each bus stop and each crossing
to the segment that contains it"]
    S1 --> S2
    S2 -- No --> S3
    S2 -- Yes --> S4 --> S5
  end

  subgraph DET["2 · Find the slow-downs"]
    direction TB
    D1["Read the speed every 2 seconds"] --> D2["Flag every instant the bus is under 5 mph as 'slow'"]
    D2 --> D3["Group consecutive 'slow' instants into candidate slow-downs
(each has a start & end time and a start & end position)"]
    D3 --> D4["Discard any slow-down shorter than 10 seconds"]
    D4 --> D5["Merge two slow-downs into one if the gap between them
is under 20 m (the bus was just creeping forward)"]
  end

  subgraph CAT["3 · Categorize EACH slow-down — tests applied in this order"]
    direction TB
    C0["Find the segment where the bus spent the MOST of this
slow-down's time — call it the 'home segment'"]
    C0b{"Found a
home segment?"}
    C0skip["Skip this slow-down"]:::stop
    Q1{"Does the slow-down's position range overlap a bus stop's
DWELL ZONE? (zone = 30 m before to 10 m after the stop,
trimmed at any intersection and at the segment edges)"}
    A1["DWELL at that stop
(earliest overlapping stop in travel order wins)"]:::cat
    Q1b{"Is that stop
near-side?"}
    A1b["Also flag 'DWELL AT A NEAR-SIDE SIGNAL'
(dwell vs. signal wait can't be separated here)"]:::cat
    Q2{"Does it overlap a non-signal CROSSING's approach?
(50 m before to 10 m after the crossing)"}
    A2["CROSSING at that crossing"]:::cat
    Q3{"Does it overlap the home segment's downstream SIGNAL
approach? (300 ft / 91 m before to 10 m after the signal)"}
    A3["SIGNAL — uniform wait — at that signal"]:::cat
    A4["SLOWDOWN — left unattributed"]:::cat
    done["this slow-down is now categorized"]:::join
    C0 --> C0b
    C0b -- No --> C0skip
    C0b -- Yes --> Q1
    Q1 -- Yes --> A1 --> Q1b
    Q1b -- Yes --> A1b --> done
    Q1b -- No --> done
    Q1 -- No --> Q2
    Q2 -- Yes --> A2 --> done
    Q2 -- No --> Q3
    Q3 -- Yes --> A3 --> done
    Q3 -- No --> A4 --> done
  end

  subgraph OVF["4 · Signal-overflow second pass"]
    direction TB
    O1["Within each segment, walk its slow-downs in time order"] --> O2["For each SIGNAL slow-down, look at the slow-downs
immediately before it"]
    O2 --> O3["Re-label each consecutive 'unattributed SLOWDOWN' as
SIGNAL — overflow — for the same signal; stop at the first
dwell / crossing / signal already labeled"]
  end

  End["OUTPUT — every slow-down now has a category and a facility,
drawn as a colored delay bar"]:::io

  S5 --> D1
  D5 --> C0
  done --> O1
  O3 --> End
